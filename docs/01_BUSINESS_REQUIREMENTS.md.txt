# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the **real-world business requirements** of the Smart Textile Business Management System. It describes how the textile business operates today and how the proposed system will improve and automate those operations.

This document is the **foundation for system design, database modeling, API development, and frontend architecture**.

---

# 2. Business Overview

The client operates a textile business that includes:

* Fabric retail sales
* Ready-made garment sales
* Custom uniform manufacturing
* Bulk institutional orders (schools, companies, hospitals)
* In-house production workflow

The business currently operates using manual tools such as:

* Paper-based order books
* Excel sheets for inventory
* WhatsApp for customer communication
* Manual billing and tracking

---

# 3. Current Business Workflow (As-Is System)

## 3.1 Customer Order Flow (Manual)

1. Customer visits store or contacts via WhatsApp
2. Staff manually records order
3. Product availability is checked manually
4. Price is calculated manually
5. Order is written in register
6. Customer is notified manually
7. No real-time tracking exists

---

## 3.2 Inventory Management (Manual Excel System)

* Stock is updated manually in Excel
* No real-time synchronization
* No automatic alerts for low stock
* Errors occur due to manual updates
* Stock reconciliation is done weekly or monthly

---

## 3.3 Production Workflow

* Orders are handed to tailors verbally or on paper
* No digital tracking of production stages
* No visibility for managers
* Delays are identified only after customer complaints

---

## 3.4 Payment Handling

* Cash payments recorded manually
* Bank transfers verified manually
* No integration with payment gateways
* No automatic reconciliation

---

## 3.5 Customer Management

* Customer details stored in notebooks or WhatsApp chats
* No centralized customer database
* No purchase history tracking
* No customer segmentation

---

# 4. Problems Identified

## 4.1 Operational Problems

* High dependency on manual work
* Human errors in order entry
* Delayed order processing
* Lack of real-time visibility
* Inefficient communication

---

## 4.2 Inventory Problems

* Frequent stock mismatches
* Overstocking of slow-moving items
* Stockouts of high-demand products
* No predictive ordering

---

## 4.3 Production Problems

* No tracking of production stages
* No accountability per worker
* No delivery estimation accuracy
* Bottlenecks not visible

---

## 4.4 Customer Experience Problems

* No online ordering system
* Slow response time
* No order tracking
* No personalization

---

## 4.5 Management Problems

* No analytics or reporting
* No forecasting
* Decisions based on intuition
* No performance metrics

---

# 5. To-Be System (Proposed Digital Workflow)

## 5.1 Customer Order Flow (Digital)

1. Customer browses products online
2. Adds products to cart
3. Places order via website
4. System validates stock
5. Order is created automatically
6. Payment is processed online
7. Order is sent to production (if custom)
8. Customer receives real-time updates

---

## 5.2 Inventory Flow

* Stock is updated automatically on every order
* Low stock alerts are generated
* Inventory is synchronized in real-time
* Purchase orders can be generated automatically

---

## 5.3 Production Flow

1. Order enters production queue
2. Tasks are created for workers
3. Each stage is tracked:

   * Cutting
   * Stitching
   * Finishing
   * Quality Check
4. Progress is updated in system
5. Delivery is marked when completed

---

## 5.4 Payment Flow

* Customers pay via online gateway (PayHere/Stripe)
* System verifies payment automatically
* Order status updates instantly
* Payment records stored securely

---

## 5.5 Customer Management Flow

* Customer accounts created automatically
* Order history stored permanently
* Measurement data stored for repeat orders
* Customer segmentation enabled (VIP, Regular, New)

---

# 7. Business Rules

* BR1: Orders cannot be confirmed without stock validation
* BR2: Payments must be verified before marking order as paid
* BR3: Custom orders require measurement data
* BR4: Inventory cannot go below zero
* BR5: Each production task must be assigned to a worker
* BR6: Order status must follow defined workflow sequence

---

# 8. Key Business Entities

* Users (Customers, Admins, Workers)
* Products (Fabric, Uniforms, Accessories)
* Orders
* Order Items
* Inventory
* Production Tasks
* Workers
* Payments
* Suppliers (future scope)

---

# 9. Business Workflow Summary

The system transforms the business into a fully digital workflow:

**Customer → Product → Cart → Order → Payment → Production → Delivery**

All steps are tracked in real-time and stored in a centralized system.

---

# 10. Success Criteria

The system will be considered successful if:

* Orders are processed without manual registers
* Inventory is updated automatically
* Production stages are trackable
* Payments are automated and verified
* Customers can order online
* Management can see real-time analytics
* Human errors are significantly reduced
